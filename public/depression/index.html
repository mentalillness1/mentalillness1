Information about Depression

What is Depression?

Depression is a common mental health condition characterized by persistent feelings of sadness, hopelessness, and loss of interest in activities. It can affect how a person thinks, feels, and functions in daily life.

What are the symptoms of Depression?

Symptoms include persistent sadness, loss of interest in previously enjoyable activities, fatigue, changes in appetite or sleep patterns, difficulty concentrating, feelings of worthlessness, and thoughts of self-harm or suicide.

How common is Depression?

Depression is one of the most common mental health conditions, affecting approximately 5% of the global population. It can affect people of all ages, genders, and backgrounds.

What causes Depression?

Depression can result from a combination of genetic, biological, environmental, and psychological factors. Stressful life events, trauma, and major life changes may also contribute to its onset.

How can I support someone with Depression?

Be supportive and patient. Encourage them to seek professional help, such as therapy or medication. Listen without judgment, and remind them they are not alone in their struggles.

Is there a stigma surrounding Depression?

Yes, despite being common, depression is often stigmatized. Increasing awareness, fostering open conversations, and educating others can help reduce the stigma and encourage people to seek help.
