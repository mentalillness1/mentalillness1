Information about PTSD

What is PTSD?

Post-Traumatic Stress Disorder (PTSD) is a mental health condition triggered by experiencing or witnessing a traumatic event.

What are the symptoms of PTSD?

Symptoms include flashbacks, nightmares, severe anxiety, and uncontrollable thoughts about the traumatic event.

How common is PTSD?

PTSD affects approximately 3-4% of the population at any given time, with higher rates in veterans and people involved in the military/war

What causes PTSD?

PTSD can be caused by experiencing or witnessing traumatic events such as natural disasters, accidents, combat, or personal assaults.

How can I support someone with PTSD?

Offer a safe and non-judgmental space for them to share their feelings. Encourage professional help like therapy or counseling, and be patient and supportive.

Is there a stigma surrounding PTSD?

Yes, but increased awareness and education are reducing the stigma surrounding PTSD.
