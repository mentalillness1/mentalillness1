Information about Panic Disorder

What is Panic Disorder?

Panic Disorder is a mental health condition characterized by recurrent and unexpected panic attacks, which are sudden episodes of intense fear or discomfort.

What are the symptoms of Panic Disorder?

Symptoms include rapid heart rate, sweating, trembling, shortness of breath, chest pain, dizziness, and a fear of losing control or dying.

How common is Panic Disorder?

Panic Disorder affects approximately 2-3% of the population, with symptoms often beginning in late adolescence or early adulthood.

What causes Panic Disorder?

Panic Disorder may be caused by a combination of genetic, biological, environmental, and psychological factors. Stress and significant life changes can also trigger it.

How can I support someone with Panic Disorder?

Provide reassurance during panic attacks, remain calm, and avoid judgment. Encourage seeking professional help and learning relaxation techniques together.

Is there a stigma surrounding Panic Disorder?

Yes, but ongoing awareness and education efforts are helping to reduce the stigma associated with mental health conditions, including Panic Disorder.
