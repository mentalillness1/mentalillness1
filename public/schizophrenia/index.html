Information about Schizophrenia

What is Schizophrenia?

Schizophrenia is a serious mental disorder that affects how a person thinks, feels, and behaves. People with schizophrenia may seem like they have lost touch with reality, experiencing symptoms like hallucinations, delusions, and disorganized thinking.

What are the symptoms of Schizophrenia?

Symptoms include delusions, hallucinations, disorganized speech, trouble with thinking and concentration, and lack of motivation or emotion (called "negative symptoms").

How common is Schizophrenia?

Schizophrenia affects approximately 1% of the global population. It typically develops in late adolescence or early adulthood, with slightly higher prevalence in men.

What causes Schizophrenia?

The exact cause of schizophrenia is unknown, but it's believed to be a combination of genetic, brain chemistry, and environmental factors. Stressful life events and drug use may trigger symptoms in vulnerable individuals.

How can I support someone with Schizophrenia?

Be patient and listen without judgment. Encourage seeking professional help, such as therapy or medication. Provide practical support for daily living and educate yourself about the disorder to better understand their experience.

Is there a stigma surrounding Schizophrenia?

Yes, schizophrenia is often misunderstood, leading to stigma. Increased awareness, education, and open dialogue are essential to reduce misconceptions and support those affected.
